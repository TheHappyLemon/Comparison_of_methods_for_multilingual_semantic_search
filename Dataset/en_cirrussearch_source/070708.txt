Kunda is a town in the Viru-Nigula Parish of Estonia, located on the coast of the Gulf of Finland. Kunda is best known for its cement factory, port, and archaeological heritage. Evidence of some of the oldest prehistoric communities in Estonia—hunting, and fishing communities that existed around 6500 BC—were found near Kunda. This settlement gave the name to the Kunda culture. The first written record of Kunda dates back to 1241, when it was mentioned as a village. In 1443 it was mentioned as a manor. Kunda was granted official borough rights on May 1, 1938. The former municipality of Kunda was twinned with: Gdynia, Poland Argo Aadli (born 1980), theatre and film actor Armin Öpik (1898–1983), paleontologist Ernst Öpik (1893–1985), astronomer and astrophysicist Jüri Parijõgi (1892–1941), writer and scholar Knudåge Riisager (1897–1974), composer, son of expatriate Danish parents (his father running the cement factory), spent his first 3 years in Kunda.
