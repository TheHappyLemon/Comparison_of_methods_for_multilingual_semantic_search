The Burmese ferret-badger (Melogale personata), also known as the large-toothed ferret-badger, is a mustelid native to Southeast Asia. The Burmese ferret-badger has a head and body length of 35–40 centimetres (14–16 in), a tail length of 15–21 centimetres (5.9–8.3 in) and a body weight of 1.5–3 kilograms (3.3–6.6 lb). The fur ranges from fawn brown to dark brown, with a white dorsal stripe. The face is marked with black and white patches, which are unique to each individual. The rear part of the tail is whitish. Three subspecies are recognized: M. p. personata, northeastern India and Bangladesh to southern Burma and Thailand M. p. nipalensis, Nepal M. p. pierrei, Cambodia, southern China, Laos and Viet Nam.
