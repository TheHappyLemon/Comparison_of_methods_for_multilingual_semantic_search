Marcel Haščák (Slovak pronunciation: [ˈmartsel ˈɦaʂtʂaːk]; born 3 February 1987) is a Slovak ice hockey player. He is currently a free agent. Haščák played junior ice hockey for his hometown club HK Poprad. He debuted at senior level in the 2005–06 season. In the 2007–08 season he earned 39 points and finished 5th in club stats. After 5 seasons for HK Poprad he signed a contract for HC Košice. He won the Slovak Extraliga title for Košice in the 2010–11 season, earning 31 points in the regular season and 4 points in the playoffs. Haščák joined Dinamo Riga on try-out in August 2013. On August 21, 2013, Haščák signed one-year contract with Dinamo Riga. Haščák was a part of the silver medal winning team at the 2012 IIHF World Championship.
