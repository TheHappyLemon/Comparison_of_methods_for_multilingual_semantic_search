Jonathan Glao Tah (German pronunciation: [ˈjoːnatan ˈtaː]; born 11 February 1996) is a German professional footballer who plays as a centre-back for Bundesliga club Bayer Leverkusen and the Germany national team. Tah was born in Hamburg, to an Ivorian father and a German mother. He grew up in the Hamburg district of Altona. Tah started playing football at local clubs Altona 93 and SC Concordia before being snapped up by Hamburger SV. On 4 August 2013, Tah made his competitive debut for Hamburg in a DFB-Pokal match against SV Schott Jena. He made his Bundesliga debut on 21 September 2013 in a 2-0 home loss against Werder Bremen. On 1 September 2014, he was loaned to Fortuna Düsseldorf for a year. On 15 July 2015 he signed a five-year contract with Bayer Leverkusen for a fee of €7.5 million. On 26 February 2018, Bayer Leverkusen announced the extension of Tah's contract with the club until 2023. He made his 100th Bundesliga appearance for the club on 10 March 2019. In September 2021, Tah signed a contract extension with the club until 2025. After playing for the Germany youth sides from U16 to U21, Tah received his first call up to the senior Germany squad in March 2016 for friendlies against England and Italy. He made his debut in the former, coming on at half-time for Mats Hummels in a 2–3 loss. He was called up to replace Antonio Rüdiger at Euro 2016. Tah was not included in the Germany squads for the 2018 World Cup or the 2022 World Cup.
