Izberbash (Russian: Изберба́ш; Dargwa: Избир; Kumyk: Йизбирба́ш, Yizbirbaş) is a town in the Republic of Dagestan, Russia, located on the coast of the Caspian Sea 56 kilometers (35 mi) southeast of Makhachkala, the capital of the republic. Population: 55,646 (2010 Census); 39,365 (2002 Census); 28,122 (1989 Census). It was founded in 1932 as an oil extraction settlement.[citation needed] Town status was granted to it in 1949.[citation needed] Within the framework of administrative divisions, it is incorporated as the Town of Izberbash—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the districts. As a municipal division, the Town of Izberbash is incorporated as Izberbash Urban Okrug. Ethnic groups in the city administrative area (2002 census): Dargins (65.4%) Kumyks (14.4%) Lezgins (7.3%) Russians (5.1%) Avars (2.8%) Laks (2.0%) Azerbaijanis (1.0%) Ethnic groups in 2021: Dargins (66.1%) Kumyks (15.7%) Lezgins (10.0%) Russians (2.1%) Avars (2.6%) Laks (1.7%) Tabasarans (0.8%) Izberbash has a cold semi-arid climate (Köppen climate classification: BSk).
