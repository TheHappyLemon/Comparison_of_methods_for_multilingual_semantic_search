Tamsalu is a town in Tapa Parish, Lääne-Viru County, Estonia. The town has a station on the Tallinn-Tartu railway. As of December 31, 2021, the town had a population of 2415. The settlement was first mentioned in 1512, but remained an insignificant village until 1876, when the Tallinn-Tartu railway was built. The settlement became a town in 1996. Before the administrative reform in 2017, the town was the administrative center of the former Tamsalu Parish.
