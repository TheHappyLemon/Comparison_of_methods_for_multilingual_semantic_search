Taurene Parish (Latvian: Taurenes pagasts) is an administrative unit of Cēsis Municipality in the Vidzeme region of Latvia. It is one of the 21 parishes in this municipality. Before the administrative reform of 2009, Taurene Parish was part of the former Cēsis District. Taurene is located in a hilly region. Land use consists mostly of agriculture, forests and lakes. The regional road P30 runs through Taureme. Hamlets in Taurene Parish are: Andreni Banuzi Brezgis Jeruzi Lodes Muiza Mezrijas Nekins Runtes Taurene Zalkalns The Gauja river flows through Taurene. A number of lakes are located in Taurene parish:[citation needed] Banuzu ezers Brenkuzu ezers Dabaru dīķis Daboru ezers Ilzes Lodes ezers Kalenites ezers Rijas ezers Stupenu ezers Taurenes ezers Zelleskaina dīķis The Nekina Manor is located in Nekins. In Taurene the Dutch environmental organization ARK is involved in a natural grazing project. In 2008 ARK introduced a herd of Konik horses to a privately owned piece of land.
