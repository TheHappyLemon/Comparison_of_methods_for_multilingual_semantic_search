Almazna (Ukrainian: Алма́зна, Russian: Алмáзная, romanized: Almaznaya) is a small city in Kadiivka Municipality, Luhansk Oblast of Ukraine. The population of Almazna was estimated to be 4,148 (2022 estimate). Industries within Almazna include metallurgy and manufacturing; a coal mine is also located near the city. Since 2014, Almazna has been under the effective control of the self-proclaimed Luhansk People's Republic. Almazna is located 65 km away from Luhansk, and 597.9 km away from Kyiv. The river Komyshuvakha originates on the eastern part of the city, while the river Lomuvatka flows 1 km west of the city. The railway station Stakhanov (formerly known as Almazna) is situated 2 km away from the city.
