Chegem (Russian: Чеге́м; Kabardian: Шэджэм; Karachay-Balkar: Чегем, Çegem) is a town and the administrative center of Chegemsky District of the Kabardino-Balkarian Republic, Russia, located 10 kilometers (6.2 mi) north of Nalchik, at the elevation of about 470 meters (1,540 ft). Population: 18,019 (2010 Census). Originally called Chegem Pervy (Чеге́м Пе́рвый), it was granted urban-type settlement status in 1972.[citation needed] In 2000, it was granted town status and renamed Chegem.[citation needed] Within the framework of administrative divisions, Chegem serves as the administrative center of Chegemsky District, to which it is directly subordinated. As a municipal division, the town of Chegem is incorporated within Chegemsky Municipal District as Chegem Urban Settlement. Population: 18,019 (2010 Census); 17,893 (2002 Census); 13,225 (1989 Census).
