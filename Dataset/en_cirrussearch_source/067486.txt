Yuzhno-Sukhokumsk (Russian: Ю́жно-Сухоку́мск, Avar: Салануб; Lezgian: Кьибле-Сухокумск) is a town in the Republic of Dagestan, Russia, located on the Sukhaya Kuma River, 295 kilometers (183 mi) northwest of Makhachkala. Population: 10,035 (2010 Census); 9,777 (2002 Census); 12,246 (1989 Census). It was granted urban-type settlement status in 1963 and town status in 1988. Within the framework of administrative divisions, it is incorporated as the Town of Yuzhno-Sukhokumsk—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the districts. As a municipal division, the Town of Yuzhno-Sukhokumsk is incorporated as Yuzhno-Sukhokumsk Urban Okrug. Ethnic groups (2002 census): Avars (47.9%) Dargins (18.5%) Lezgins (10.7%) Laks (9.0%) Russians (5.4%) Kumyks (3.8%) Yuzhno-Sukhokumsk has a cold semi-arid climate (Köppen climate classification: BSk).
