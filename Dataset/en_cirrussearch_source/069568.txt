The Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Comedy or Musical is a Golden Globe Award that was first awarded by the Hollywood Foreign Press Association as a separate category in 1951. Previously, there was a single award for "Best Actress in a Motion Picture", but the splitting allowed for recognition of it and the Best Actress – Drama. The formal title has varied since its inception. In 2005, it was officially called "Best Performance by an Actress in a Motion Picture – Comedy or Musical". As of 2014[update], the wording is "Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Comedy or Musical".   indicates the winner Angela Bassett became the first actress of African descent to win when she won in 1993. Marion Cotillard became the first actress to win for a foreign-language/non-english language performance when she won in 2007. Awkwafina became the first actress of Asian descent to win when she won in 2020. Rachel Zegler became the first Latina actress to win as well the youngest winner in that category at 20 years old when she won in 2022. Michelle Yeoh became the first Malaysian actress to win when she won in 2023.
