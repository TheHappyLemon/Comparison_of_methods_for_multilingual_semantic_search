Buda-Kashalyova (Belarusian: Буда-Кашалёва; Russian: Буда-Кошелёво, romanized: Buda-Koshelyovo) is a town in Gomel Region, Belarus. It serves as the administrative center of Buda-Kashalyova District. As of 2023, it has a population of 8,618. It was first mentioned in chronicles from the first half of 1824 as village Buda in Mogilev Governorate. Its population was 500 (1890s). During World War II, Buda-Kashalyova was under German occupation from 15 August 1941 until 27 November 1943.
