Cirsti Manor (Latvian: Cirstu muižas pils, German: Zirstenhoff) is a two-storey manor house built in 1886 in Neo-Gothic red brick style in the historical region of Vidzeme, northern Latvia. Unusual to Neo-Gothic structures in Latvia, the hexagonal three-storey manor tower is detached from the main building.
