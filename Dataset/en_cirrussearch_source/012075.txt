Year 1332 (MCCCXXXII) was a leap year starting on Wednesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar. February 18 – Amda Seyon I, Emperor of Ethiopia, begins his campaigns in the southern Muslim provinces (possibly in 1329). August 10–11 – Battle of Dupplin Moor: Edward Balliol rebels, and the English defeat the loyalists of David II in Scotland. September – Edward Balliol crowns himself King of Scotland. November 7 – Lucerne joins the Swiss Confederation with Uri, Schwyz, and Unterwalden. December 16 – Battle of Annan: The loyalists of David II defeat Edward Balliol in Scotland. The city of Marosvásárhely (in Transylvania, today Târgu Mureș in Romania) is first documented in the papal registry, under the name Novum Forum Siculorum.
