71 (seventy-one) is the natural number following 70 and preceding 72. Look up seventy-one in Wiktionary, the free dictionary. Because both rearrangements of its digits (17 and 71) are prime numbers, 71 is an emirp and more generally a permutable prime. It is the largest number which occurs as a prime factor of an order of a sporadic simple group, the largest (15th) supersingular prime. It is a Pillai prime, since 9!+1{\displaystyle 9!+1} is divisible by 71, but 71 is not one more than a multiple of 9.
