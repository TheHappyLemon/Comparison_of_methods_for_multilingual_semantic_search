Geranium phaeum, commonly called dusky crane's-bill, mourning widow or black widow, is a herbaceous plant species in the family Geraniaceae. It is native to southern, central, and western Europe, and is cultivated as a garden subject. It has dark violet colored flowers. It is unmistakable with dark purple petals turned backwards and with conspicuous projecting stamens and style. Petals crinkly-edged and pointed. Leaves often blotched brown. The cultivar 'Our Pat' has gained the Royal Horticultural Society's Award of Garden Merit. BSBI List 2007 (xls). Botanical Society of Britain and Ireland.
