Year 1363 (MCCCLXIII) was a common year starting on Sunday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar. April 9 – Haakon VI of Norway marries Margaret I of Denmark. August – The Revolt of Saint Titus, against the rule of the Republic of Venice in the Kingdom of Candia (island of Crete), begins. August 30–October 4 – Battle of Lake Poyang: The Dahan rebel forces of Chen Youliang are defeated by the Red Turban Rebel forces of Zhu Yuanzhang, during the final decade of Yuan Dynasty control over China. Zhu's naval forces of 200,000 are pitted against Chen's naval forces of 650,000 troops, in what is not only the largest naval battle of the medieval age, but also one of the largest naval battles in history. Byzantine–Ottoman wars Ottoman Turks seize Filibe (Philippopolis) in Thrace. The Byzantine Empire wins a naval battle over the Ottoman Empire near Megara, Greece.[citation needed] Dmitry Donskoy, Prince of the Grand Duchy of Moscow, dethrones Dmitry of Suzdal to become Grand Prince of Vladimir. Philip the Bold becomes duke of Burgundy. Al-Afdal al-Abbas succeeds Al-Mujahid Ali as Rasulid Sultan of Yemen. The Mosque-Madrassa of Sultan Hassan is completed in Cairo, Egypt.
