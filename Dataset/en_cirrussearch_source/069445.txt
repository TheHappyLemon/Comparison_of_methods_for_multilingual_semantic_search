The Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Drama is a Golden Globe Award that was first awarded by the Hollywood Foreign Press Association as a separate category in 1951. Previously, there was a single award for "Best Actress in a Motion Picture", but the splitting allowed for recognition of it and the Best Actress – Comedy or Musical. The formal title has varied since its inception. In 2005, it was officially called "Best Performance by an Actress in a Motion Picture – Drama". As of 2013, the wording is "Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Drama".   indicates the winner Anouk Aimée became the first actress to win for a foreign language/non-english language performance in 1967. Whoopi Goldberg became the first actress of African descent to win in 1986. Marlee Matlin became the first deaf actress to win in 1987. Lily Gladstone became the first actress of Native American heritage to win in 2024.
