Greyhounds (Portuguese: Os Galgos) is a painting by Amadeo de Souza Cardoso, from 1911. The painting is an oil on canvas with overall dimensions of 100 x 73 centimeters. It is in the collection of the Centro de Arte Moderna Gulbenkian, in Lisbon. The scene shows two greyhounds and a hare against a flat background. João B. Serra; Schirn Kunsthalle Frankfurt; Centro Cultural de Belém (Lisbon, Portugal) (1998). Modern art in Portugal: 1910-1940 : the artist contemporaries of Fernando Pessoa. Edition Stemmle.
