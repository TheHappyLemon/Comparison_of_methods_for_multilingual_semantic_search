Year 1083 (MLXXXIII) was a common year starting on Sunday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar. January 6 – A Castilian army, under Count Gonzalo Salvadórez and his son-in-law Ramiro Garcés, Lord of Calahorra, child of the late King García Sánchez III of Pamplona, enters the surrendered bastian of Rueda, but are then treacherously set upon and killed. Gonzalo, Ramiro, and Ramiro's illegitimate half-brother Sancho Garcés are among the many nobles to lose their lives, in what will be remembered as the 'disaster' or 'treachery of Rueda'. Castilian forces under Alfonso VI reconquer Talavera de la Reina in the Taifa of Toledo (modern Spain). King Sancho Ramírez of Pamplona and Aragon, conquers Graus (located in the Pyrenees). Summer – Emperor Henry IV besieges Pope Gregory VII in Castel Sant'Angelo at Rome. October – While Robert Guiscard is in Italy, emperor Alexios I Komnenos retakes territory previously lost to the Normans, including the town of Kastoria. Several Normans, including Peter Aliphas, switch sides to the Byzantines. King William I (the Conqueror) imprisons his half-brother Odo of Bayeux for planning a military expedition to Italy. Ceuta falls to the Almoravids, after a five-year siege.
