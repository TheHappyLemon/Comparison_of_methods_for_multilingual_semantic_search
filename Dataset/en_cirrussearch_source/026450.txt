The Balearic Sea (endotoponym: Mar Balear in Catalan and Spanish) also known as Iberian Sea, is a body of water in the Mediterranean Sea between the Balearic Islands and mainland Spain. The Ebro River flows into this small sea. The Balearic islands are divided into two groups: Gimnesias in the northeast, and Pitiusas in the southwest.
