Latvian Football Cup 2009–10 was the sixty-eighth season of the Latvian annual football knock-out competition. For the first time the cup season switched from calendar year to fall/spring season. The winners, Jelgava, qualified for the second qualifying round of the UEFA Europa League 2010–11. The eight winners from the previous round compete in this round. These matches took place on 14 April 2010. The four winners from the previous round compete in this round.
