Klaksvíkar Ítróttarfelag ("Klaksvík Sports Club"), commonly abbreviated to KÍ or KÍ Klaksvik, is a Faroese professional football organisation based in Klaksvík. The club was founded in 1904 and is one of the most successful Faroese football clubs, having won the Faroe Islands Premier League twenty-one times and the Faroe Islands Cup six times. The club wears blue and white and plays matches at the Við Djúpumýrar stadium. KÍ Klaksvik notably became the first Faroese football club to play in the group stage of a major UEFA-organised European competition, qualfiying for this phase in the 2023–24 edition of the UEFA Europa Conference League.
