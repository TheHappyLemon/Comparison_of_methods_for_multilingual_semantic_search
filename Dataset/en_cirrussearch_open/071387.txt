Naberezhnye Chelny (Russian: На́бережные Челны́; Tatar: Яр Чаллы (Yar Chally), IPA: [ˈjar ɕɑlːɤ̆]) is the second largest city in the Republic of Tatarstan, Russia. A major industrial center, Naberezhnye Chelny stands on the Kama River 225 kilometers (140 mi) east of Kazan near Nizhnekamsk Reservoir. Population: 548,434 (2021 Census); 513,193 (2010 Census); 509,870 (2002 Census); 500,309 (1989 Census). The city was briefly known as Brezhnev from 1982 to 1988.
