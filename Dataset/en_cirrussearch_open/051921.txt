He operated a fur trading post at Carroll, Montana 47°34′25″N 108°22′24″W﻿ / ﻿47.57361°N 108.37333°W﻿ / 47.57361; -108.37333﻿ (Carroll, Montana) and lived among the Pikuni tribe during the period 1880-82. He was given the name Apikuni by the Pikuni chief, Running Crane. Apikuni in Blackfeet means "Spotted Robe." Schultz is most noted for his 37 books, most about Blackfoot life, and for his contributions to the naming of prominent features in Glacier National Park.
