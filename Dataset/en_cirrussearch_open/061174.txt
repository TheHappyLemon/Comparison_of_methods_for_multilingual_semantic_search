Antonio Candreva (Italian pronunciation: [anˈtɔːnjo kanˈdrɛːva]; born 28 February 1987) is an Italian professional footballer who plays as a right midfielder or right winger for Serie A club Salernitana. Candreva began his professional club career with Ternana in 2004, where he remained until 2007. He later moved to Udinese, remaining until 2012, but was sent on loan to Livorno, Juventus, Parma, and Cesena during his time with the club. He subsequently transferred to Lazio, where he won the Coppa Italia in his first season with the team. He joined Inter Milan in 2016, and made over 150 appearances across four seasons at the club. At international level, Candreva represented Italy at the 2008 Summer Olympics, 2013 FIFA Confederations Cup (winning a bronze medal), 2014 FIFA World Cup, and UEFA Euro 2016.
