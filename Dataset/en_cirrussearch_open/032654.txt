The Et'hem Bey Mosque (Albanian: Xhamia e Haxhi Et'hem Beut, Turkish: HacÄ± Edhem Bey Camii) is a mosque in Tirana, Albania. Closed under communist rule, the mosque reopened as a house of worship in 1991. Without permission from the authorities, 10,000 people attended and the police did not interfere. Frescoes outside and inside the portico depict trees, waterfalls and bridges.
