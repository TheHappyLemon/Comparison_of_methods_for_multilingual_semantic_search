Robert Ley (German: [ˈlaɪ]; 15 February 1890 – 25 October 1945) was a German politician and labour and trade union leader during the Nazi era, who headed the German Labour Front from 1933 to 1945. He also held many other high positions in the German Nazi Party, including Gauleiter, Reichsleiter and Reichsorganisationsleiter. He committed suicide while awaiting trial at Nuremberg for crimes against humanity and war crimes.
