Yuri Petrovich Gaven (Russian: Ю́рий Петро́вич Гаве́н; Latvian: Juris Gavēnis; 18 March 1884 – 4 October 1936), born Jānis Daumanis (Russian: Ян Эрнестович Дауман), was a Latvian revolutionary and Soviet politician and Chekist. He was a key figure in the defeat of the Crimean People's Republic and the establishment of the short-lived Taurida Soviet Socialist Republic and an active participant in the Red Terror in Crimea. Executed during the Stalinist purges in 1936, he was rehabilitated in 1958.
