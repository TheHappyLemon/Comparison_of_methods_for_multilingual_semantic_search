Chachersk (Belarusian: Чачэрск, romanized: Čačersk, IPA: [tʂaˈtʂɛrsk]; Russian: Чечерск, romanized: Chechersk; Polish: Czeczersk) is a town in Gomel Region, Belarus. It serves as the administrative center of Chachersk District. As of 2023, it has a population of 8,885. It is located in an area which was highly contaminated due to the fallout of the Chernobyl disaster.
