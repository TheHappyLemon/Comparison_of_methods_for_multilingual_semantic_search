The UCI Road World Cup was a season-long road cycling competition held from 1989 until 2004 and comprising ten one-day events. The World Cup was made up of around ten one-day races chosen from the prestigious classics. An individual classification and a team classification were established. In the last editions, the first 25 in each round scored from 100 to 1 points. During these events, the provisional leader of the classification wore a distinctive jersey.
