Ugolnye Kopi (Russian: У́гольные Ко́пи, lit. coal mines) is an urban locality (an urban-type settlement) in Anadyrsky District of Chukotka Autonomous Okrug, Russia, located east of Anadyr, the administrative center of the autonomous okrug, on the opposite side of the Anadyr River. It served as the administrative center of Anadyrsky District until June 2011. Population: 3,368 (2010 Census); 3,863 (2002 Census); 12,357 (1989 Census), with an estimated population as of 1 January 2015 of 3,666. It is a mainly mining town that also hosts the region's main airport.
