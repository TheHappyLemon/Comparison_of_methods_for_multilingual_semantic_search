56°51′55″N 16°38′06″E﻿ / ﻿56.86516667°N 16.63488889°E﻿ / 56.86516667; 16.63488889 Solliden Palace -- commonly known as just Solliden -- is the summer residence of the Swedish Royal Family and the personal private property of King Carl XVI Gustaf.[citation needed] The palace is situated near the Borgholm Castle ruin on the island of Öland in southern Sweden along its Baltic coast. The Royal Family uses the palace for celebrating Victoriadagen.
