Conakry (/ﾋkﾉ地ﾉ冖ri/; French pronunciation: [kﾉ馬akﾊ（]; Susu: Kﾉ馬akiri; N'Ko: ﾟ槧節｣ﾟ玩槧听午ｫ, Fula: 椄択椄ｮ椄ｲ椄｢棗椄ｳ椄ｭ椄ｪ椄ｭ, romanized:ﾂKonaakiri) is the capital and largest city of Guinea. A port city, it serves as the economic, financial and cultural centre of Guinea. Its population as of the 2014 Guinea census was 1,660,973. The current population of Conakry is difficult to ascertain, although the U.S. Department of State's Bureau of African Affairs has estimated it at two million, accounting for one-sixth of the entire population of the country.
