Stanisław Bułak-Bałachowicz (12 November 1883 – 10 May 1940) was a general, military commander and veteran of World War I, the Russian Civil War, Estonian War of Independence, the 1918–1921 Polish-Soviet War, and the 1939 Invasion of Poland at the start of World War II. He is remembered as a national hero of the Belarusian opposition and of the Belarusian minority in Poland.
