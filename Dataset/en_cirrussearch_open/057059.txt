|date=July 2014}} The Nerodimka (Serbian Cyrillic: Неродимка, Albanian: Nerodimja or Nerodime) is a river in the Nerodime region of Kosovo, a 41 km-long left tributary to the Lepenc river. It represents Europe's only instance of a river bifurcation flowing into two seas, dividing into two irreversible branches. The left branch flows into the Black Sea, and the right branch flows into the Aegean Sea. For other cases see List of unusual drainage systems.
