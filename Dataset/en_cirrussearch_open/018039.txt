Fun (stylized as fun.) is an American pop rock band based in New York City. The band consisted of Jack Antonoff (of Steel Train and Bleachers), Andrew Dost (of Anathallo and Metal Bubble Trio), and Nate Ruess (of the Format). Fun formed in 2008, and their debut studio album, Aim and Ignite, was released in 2009 to moderate commercial success. The band rose to prominence with the release of their second album, Some Nights, in 2012. The album peaked at number three on the U.S. Billboard 200 chart, and topped Billboard's Top Alternative Albums and Top Rock Albums charts. At the 55th Annual Grammy Awards, they won the award for Song of the Year for their song "We Are Young"; they also won the award for Best New Artist.
