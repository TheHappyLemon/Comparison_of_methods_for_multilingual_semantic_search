Marc Savard (born July 17, 1977) is a Canadian former professional ice hockey centre and current assistant coach with the Calgary Flames of the National Hockey League (NHL). He was drafted by the New York Rangers with the 91st overall in the 1995 NHL Entry Draft. During his NHL career Savard played for the Rangers, Calgary Flames, Atlanta Thrashers and Boston Bruins. He was an assistant coach for the St. Louis Blues during the 2019–20 season. Savard's career ended late in the 2010–11 season due to post-concussion syndrome. He did not formally announce his retirement until the conclusion of his contract with Boston after the 2016–17 season.
