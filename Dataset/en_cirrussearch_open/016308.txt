Jaungulbene Manor (Latvian: Jaungulbenes mui≈æas pils; German: Schloss Neu-Schwanenburg) is a manor house in the historical region of Vidzeme, in northern Latvia. It was built in Tudor neo-Gothic style and completed in 1878. In the 1920s Jaungulbene Manor was nationalized in accordance with Latvian Land Reform of 1920. From 1927 until the 1990s the building housed an agricultural school.
