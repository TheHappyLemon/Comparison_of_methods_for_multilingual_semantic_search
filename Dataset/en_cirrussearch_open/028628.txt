Nokia 5500 Sport is a smartphone running Symbian v9.1 operating system and the S60 3rd Edition user interface, announced on May 10, 2006. This was the first Nokia handset ever to feature text to speech and motion sensor features. Features include: Built-in 3D accelerometer: allows phone to act as pedometer/step counter. Swap key for quick one-key switching between phone, music, and sports modes. Stainless steel body: Built to resist knocks, dust and water splashes. Text to speech: Software to read aloud SMS and exercise data. Sports tracking: plans, records, and schedules workout sessions. Music: Unique tap commands for playing MP3 files. Integrated 2-megapixel camera with 4x digital zoom.
