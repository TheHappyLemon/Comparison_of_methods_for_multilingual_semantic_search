El Salvador first competed in the Olympic Games at the 1968 Summer Olympics in Mexico City, Mexico. It has participated in every Games of the Olympiad since that time, excluding those held in 1976 and 1980, when the nation joined the American-led boycott in protest of the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan.[circular reference] The nation has never participated in the Olympic Winter Games and has not earned a medal at any Games of the Olympiad. Athletes from El Salvador have represented their nation in 13 sports. A total of 118 Salvadoran athletes have competed at the Olympic Games. Swimming (28 athletes) and athletics (27 athletes) have fetched the most participation from El Salvador. The Comité Olímpico de El Salvador was created in 1925 and recognized by the International Olympic Committee in 1938.
