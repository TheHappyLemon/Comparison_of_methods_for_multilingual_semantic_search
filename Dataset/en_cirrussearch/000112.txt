Group D of the 2010 FIFA World Cup began on 13 June and ended on 23 June 2010. The group consisted of Germany, Australia, Serbia and Ghana. Along with Group G, it was considered to be a group of death. Source: FIFA Rules for classification: Tie-breaking criteria Germany advanced to play England (runners-up of Group C) in the round of 16. Ghana advanced to play United States (winners of Group C) in the round of 16. All times local (UTC+2) 13 June 2010 16:00 Loftus Versfeld Stadium, Pretoria Attendance: 38,833 Referee: Héctor Baldassi (Argentina) 13 June 2010 20:30 Moses Mabhida Stadium, Durban Attendance: 62,660 Referee: Marco Rodríguez (Mexico) Serbia went ahead in the 38th minute with a goal from winger Milan Jovanović. In the 59th minute, Serbia centre-back Nemanja Vidić was booked for a handball in the penalty box, but the ensuing penalty kick from Lukas Podolski was saved by goalkeeper Vladimir Stojković.
