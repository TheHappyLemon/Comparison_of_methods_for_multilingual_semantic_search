Cape Bird (77°10′S 166°41′E﻿ / ﻿77.167°S 166.683°E﻿ / -77.167; 166.683) is a cape which marks the north extremity of Ross Island in Antarctica. It was discovered in 1841 by a British expedition under James Clark Ross, and named by him after Lieutenant Edward J. Bird of the ship HMS Erebus. Cape Bird Hut (77°13′05″S 166°26′09″E﻿ / ﻿77.218088°S 166.435795°E﻿ / -77.218088; 166.435795) is a shelter, built in 1966 with the name of Harrison Laboratory, in order to give a facility to the researchers working at Cape Bird. The hut, which can accommodate six people, was built at Scott Base during the winter and lifted by helicopter to Cape Bird.
