The Kim are a people of Chad, who mainly inhabit four villages in the Mayo-Kebbi Est region. The 1993 RGPH census reported a total population of 15,354 in Chad. Principal economic activities include cultivation of finger millet, taro, and rice, fishing, and pottery.[citation needed] The Kim are mainly Christians (c. 65%), but many of them also profess their own tribal beliefs (c. 35%). v t e
